The Bank of Punjab — Housing Guarantee (Apni Chhatt Apna Ghar / ACAG)

Credit Proposal · Portfolio-Level Credit Guarantee (Jul 2026)

Rationale — Mandate Fit: Housing finance converts long-term rental expenditure into home ownership and asset-building for low- and moderate-income households — squarely within NCGCL's financial-inclusion mandate. The intervention is timely against the recent federal budget's affordable-housing priorities, and construction financing is one of the more job-intensive uses of guarantee capacity in the current pipeline (see SRQ jobs model below), a factor that consistently draws guarantee attraction under the Board's sector-priority screen.

Guarantee Coverage
Second-Loss, attaching after Government of Punjab's First-Loss band of up to 9% (PKR 5,400 Mn on the PKR 60,000 Mn covered portfolio) is exhausted; covers 100% of losses thereafter up to the PKR 1,000 Mn ceiling, on a disbursed-portfolio basis. Covers ETB and NTB borrowers under ACAG.
Policy Deviations / Key Covenants
None observed relative to Guarantee Policy 2025.
  • CAR > 12%
  • Facility Offer Letter: 4-year Guarantee Tenor with a 48-month utilization window runs from the effective date (i.e. facility offer letter acceptance)
  • BOP retains full autonomy over loan origination, underwriting and disbursement pacing within the agreed SOP and eligibility criteria; NCGCL's covenants govern reporting and claims, not day-to-day origination decisions
De-Risking — How the Risk Diminishes: This remains a Second-Loss facility: NCGCL absorbs no loss until the Government of Punjab's First-Loss layer (PKR 5,400 Mn) is fully depleted. As the portfolio amortizes over the 9-year programme tenor, the outstanding balance declines while the Government of Punjab's PKR 5,400 Mn first-loss cover stays fixed in absolute terms — so that fixed cover comes to represent a progressively larger share of the (shrinking) outstanding book. This steadily reduces the likelihood that losses ever reach far enough to trigger NCGCL's second-loss layer at all.
As the red line (outstanding balance) falls toward the flat gold line (fixed GoPb cover), the first-loss layer covers an ever-larger proportion of the remaining book — pushing NCGCL's attachment point further out of reach over time.
