Executive Summary
Transaction Overview
Renewable EPC Finance · Partial Credit Guarantee Facility · NCGCL

NCGCL proposes to structure a blended finance partial credit guarantee facility to unlock private capital for Engineering, Procurement & Construction (EPC) contractors operating in Pakistan's renewable energy sector. Green Climate Fund (GCF) capital of US$ 25M, matched by NCGCL's own equity of US$ 5M, generates a combined guarantee capital pool of US$ 30M. Applying a 3× guarantee leverage multiple, NCGCL deploys a guarantee envelope of US$ 90M. At a disciplined 25% partial risk cover, this mobilises total financing of up to US$ 360M — a GCF leverage multiple of >14.4×.

Market Problem Being Solved EPC contractors developing utility-scale renewable energy for Tier-1 Pakistani corporates face a structural financing gap: they lack the collateral base to support large-ticket Letters of Credit (LCs) and Bank Guarantees (BGs) required for equipment import during the construction phase. Without trade finance, projects cannot commence. NCGCL's partial guarantee covers this contingent exposure, enabling bank issuance of non-funded trade facilities without requiring EPC collateral that does not exist at this stage of the project lifecycle.
Climate Finance Thesis A 25-25-50 capital split across solar, wind, and BESS generates ~130 MW of solar PV, ~72 MW of onshore wind, and ~1,285 MWh of BESS — for Tier-1 corporate offtakers under 7–10 year PPAs. At current South Asia EPC benchmarks, this avoids approximately 258,000 tCO2/year and ~6.5 million tCO2 over a 25-year project life.

Transaction Phases, Mechanics & Leverage
Four-Phase Execution Mechanism
Sequential structure from capital mobilisation to debt settlement
1
Guarantee Capital Mobilisation
GCF (US$ 25M) and NCGCL own equity (US$ 5M), alongside KRN anchor capital, are deployed as guarantee capital. NCGCL applies 3× leverage to generate US$ 90M of guarantee coverage capacity — not a cash deployment but a contingent liability priced by expected loss, consistent with NCGCL’s AAA-rated capital structure.
2
Non-Funded Trade Finance Phase
NCGCL extends partial guarantees (25% risk cover) to a commercial bank, enabling issuance of Letters of Credit and Bank Guarantees to EPC contractors for equipment import — solar panels, wind turbines, inverters, BESS units. The guarantee covers the bank’s contingent exposure, enabling facility issuance at scale without requiring EPC collateral that does not exist at this stage of the project lifecycle.
3
Funded Take-Out — Project Finance Phase
On project commissioning, the non-funded trade facility is taken out by a funded term loan extended to a ring-fenced SPV housing all project companies (P1 through Pn). NCGCL’s partial guarantee continues to cover 25% of the funded exposure. The SPV executes PPAs with Tier-1 corporate offtakers; PPA cash flows service the funded facility over 7–10 years to full settlement.
4
Capital Market Broadening
NCGCL’s AAA-rated partial guarantee enables the SPV’s funded facility to access capital market instruments (CPs, Sukuks, bonds), broadening the investor base to insurance companies, DFIs, mutual funds, pension funds, and retail investors — reducing cost of capital and deepening Pakistan’s renewable energy financing market beyond bank balance sheets.

Additionality & GCF Mandate Alignment
Additionality

Without NCGCL’s guarantee, commercial banks are unwilling to issue trade facilities against EPC contractors who lack the collateral base to support large-ticket LC/BG requirements. The facility creates new financing flows — not cheaper financing of existing deals, but entirely new renewable energy infrastructure that would not otherwise proceed at these ticket sizes and tenors.

Private Capital Crowd-In

Every US$ 1 of GCF capital attracts US$ 14.4 of private financing from banks, insurance companies, DFIs, pension funds, and mutual funds. The facility structurally broadens Pakistan’s renewable energy capital base beyond bank balance sheets — a market-building impact that outlasts the individual facility and establishes a replicable template for future blended finance structures.

Climate & GCF Alignment

~130 MW solar, ~72 MW wind, and ~1,285 MWh BESS avoids ~258,000 tCO²/year and ~6.5 million tCO² over 25 years — directly aligned with Pakistan’s NDCs and GCF climate result area objectives. GCF-mandated M&V frameworks and emission reduction KPIs embedded from facility inception.

Institutional Thesis

Not larger guarantee volume alone, but structures that channel capital beyond bank balance sheets.

NCGCL’s Renewable EPC Finance Facility is not a subsidy mechanism — it is a catalytic finance structure that preserves underwriting discipline, enforces layered risk-sharing, and mobilises private capital at a scale that concessional capital alone cannot achieve. Every US$ 1 of GCF capital does the work of US$ 14.4 in financed renewable infrastructure: ~130 MW solar, ~72 MW wind, ~1,285 MWh BESS, and ~258,000 tCO²/year avoided. The facility is priced by expected loss, secured by PPA cash flows from Tier-1 corporate offtakers, and governed by the same institutional rigour that earned NCGCL its AAA / A1+ (PACRA) rating. Catalytic finance works only when structure preserves scarce risk capital and enforces lender accountability.
