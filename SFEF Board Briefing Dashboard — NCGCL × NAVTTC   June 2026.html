The Problem: PFIs Will Not Lend to Skills Borrowers Unaided

Pakistan's formal financial sector extends virtually no unsecured credit to individuals for professional training, certification, or vocational programs. Borrowers lack collateral; PFIs lack the credit frameworks to assess skills-linked repayment capacity; and outcomes data to calibrate risk is non-existent. The result: a structural financing vacuum that forces prospective workers either into informal debt or out of high-value training programs entirely.

NAVTTC identifies this gap as the single largest constraint on Pakistan's productive workforce expansion. Without financing access, NAVTTC's programmatic investments in course accreditation and placement infrastructure fail to translate into durable employment outcomes at scale.

The SFEF Solution: Catalytic Guarantee Capital

SFEF is a ring-fenced guarantee fund, jointly capitalized by NAVTTC and NCGCL, that enables Participating Financial Institutions (PFIs) to extend credit for approved skills programs by covering 50% of each loan's credit risk. This partial guarantee structure aligns incentives: PFIs retain meaningful skin-in-the-game while the credit risk barrier is lowered sufficiently to unlock lending.

NCGCL acts as Fund Manager and Guarantor — deploying its AAA-rated investment infrastructure, its Master Agreement on Guarantee Facility Arrangement (MAGFA) with PFIs, and its credit analytics capability. NAVTTC's returnable capital contribution is protected by NCGCL's matching capital and by investment income, which serves as a first-loss buffer before any principal is touched.

Board Consideration
NAVTTC's contribution is returnable after five years, net of any crystallized losses. Investment income accumulated over the fund's life is applied first against any claims — meaning capital is the last line of defense, not the first. At base-case assumptions, the capital base is expected to remain fully intact at end of term.

Key Structural Protections
1. Income First: All claims are absorbed from accumulated investment income before any capital is touched.  •  2. Leverage Cap: Total guarantee exposure cannot exceed 3.0× Capital Base; new issuances are halted if this is breached.  •  3. Pari Passu: NCGCL and NAVTTC bear losses proportionately — neither is senior to the other.  •  4. Returnable Capital: NAVTTC's contribution reverts after 5 years, net of any net crystallized losses.  •  5. No Principal Touch for Fees: Management fees are deducted from investment income only — never from capital principal.

Investment Management

Capital is invested per the NCGCL Investment Policy — the same policy that underpins NCGCL's PACRA AAA rating. Assets are deployed exclusively in high-quality, liquid instruments:

  • Government of Pakistan T-bills and PIBs
  • Sovereign-guaranteed instruments
  • AAA-rated corporate sukuks and term finance certificates
  • Short-tenor bank deposits at scheduled commercial banks
Liquidity Priority
The investment portfolio is managed to maintain sufficient liquidity to honor claims within the MAGFA settlement window. Liquidity takes precedence over yield maximization.

Claims Absorption Waterfall
Layer 1: Investment Income

All claims are first absorbed from cumulative net investment income since Fund inception. As long as the income buffer exceeds cumulative claims, capital principal is fully protected.

First Loss Buffer
Layer 2: Capital Base

Only when cumulative claims exceed cumulative net income does the Capital Base absorb losses — pari passu between NAVTTC and NCGCL tranches proportionate to contribution.

Second Loss
Subrogation & Recovery

Upon paying a claim, NCGCL is subrogated to the PFI's rights against the defaulting borrower. All recoveries are credited back to the Fund pro-rata, net of collection costs.

Ineligible Claims — Key Exclusions
Claims shall not be honored for: (a) penal or default interest charges; (b) fraudulent or fictitious disbursements; (c) loans extended for programs not on the SFEF Eligible Program Register at time of disbursement; (d) loans to borrowers who failed the Skills Credit Scorecard and were approved in breach of underwriting criteria; (e) claims filed without documented collection and recovery attempts by the PFI.

Board Decisions Required — Conditions Precedent to CFMGA Effectiveness
01
NAVTTC Contribution Quantum
The Board must approve the specific PKR contribution amount from NAVTTC. Currently indicated as [●]M in the CFMGA. The term sheet indicated PKR 200M. Confirmation of this quantum is a condition precedent to Agreement effectiveness. Subsequent matching tranches by NCGCL are case-by-case and require separate Board approval.
02
NCGCL Initial Matching Contribution
Board approval required for NCGCL's initial matching contribution of up to PKR 200M from NCGCL's own capital. This is a material outlay of institutional capital and constitutes a capital commitment requiring Board resolution. Timing of disbursement to be coordinated with NAVTTC's contribution.
03
Management Fee Rate
The annual management fee rate [●]% on the average Fund Account balance is a commercial term requiring Board approval. This is an NCGCL revenue item, payable from Investment Income only. Recommended range: 1.0% – 2.0% p.a., subject to negotiation with NAVTTC. Fee review is non-retrospective and effective from next quarter post-agreement.
04
Guarantee Fee Rate
The guarantee fee [●]% charged to PFIs on the guaranteed amount — per Annexure A and the MAGFA — requires Board approval. This is a separate commercial term from the Management Fee and flows to NCGCL from PFIs. Recommended range: 1.5% – 3.5% on guaranteed amount, structured as an upfront or annual fee.
05
Annexure C — Product Risk Assessment Criteria
No guarantee may be issued under SFEF until Annexure C is fully negotiated, executed, and attached to the CFMGA. Annexure C specifies: borrower eligibility criteria, provider accreditation standards, the Skills Credit Scorecard, underwriting parameters by product window, PAR trigger responses, claims documentation requirements, and exclusion criteria. Board approval of Annexure C, or delegation of approval authority to management, is required.
Fund Governance Structure
Coordination Committee
Semi-annual meetings. Advisory role only. Decisions binding only when confirmed in writing per CFMGA amendment procedure. Neither party has unilateral override authority.
NAVTTC Audit Rights
15 Business Days' notice required. Audit cost borne by NAVTTC unless material breach is found, in which case costs transfer to NCGCL. Scope limited to Fund Account records.
Reporting Obligations
Quarterly Fund Management Reports to NAVTTC (within 15 business days of quarter-end). Annual Audited Report. Live Eligible Program Register maintained by NCGCL.
