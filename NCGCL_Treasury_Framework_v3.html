7. Trading Ideas — Week of 12 Jul 2026 v3 · corrected-compliance basis · CEO sign-off required

Recommendation 1 — Concentration Management

Manage the COI position ahead of the 31 July maturity

The COI placement represents 14.05% of the portfolio, approaching the 15% ceiling applicable to AAA-rated DFI placements (or in excess of the 10% ceiling should the counterparty be rated AA+). It is recommended that, at the 31 July rollover, management either (a) confirm the counterparty's credit rating to validate continued placement at the current size, or (b) redirect a portion of the proceeds into STS or bank placements to maintain compliance irrespective of the rating outcome.

Recommendation 2 — Yield Enhancement

Expand STS allocation within available headroom

Short-Term Sukuk placements (Liberty, Daewoo, Prema) currently generate a materially higher rate premium than sovereign and DFI holdings. Combined non-sovereign exposure stands at approximately 20.9% against a 30% ceiling, representing approximately 9 percentage points of unused capacity. It is recommended that management pursue incremental STS allocation, subject to confirmation of issuer credit ratings and sector/issue-size concentration limits.

Recommendation 3 — Forward Planning

Plan the reinvestment of maturing legacy positions

Four MTB tranches totalling approximately PKR 2,585.7mn are locked at rates of 10.55%–10.75% through February–March 2027, reflecting yields contracted prior to the current rate environment. It is recommended that these maturities be reinvested at prevailing market rates in shorter tenors, rather than defaulting to a further one-year commitment.

Basis for recommendations: Recommendation 1 addresses an active compliance consideration ahead of a near-term maturity. Recommendation 2 is an opportunity to deploy existing headroom toward higher-yielding instruments, subject to the stated data gating conditions. Recommendation 3 is a forward-looking planning item requiring no immediate action.

Rate Scenario Simulations

Standing component of this framework — refreshed each cycle against the then-current portfolio structure, duration profile, and PVBP.

Yields −50bps

Moderate duration extension

Prioritize locking in 6M–1Y T-Bill paper at upcoming auctions ahead of the anticipated decline, rather than rolling into further 3M paper. The portfolio's current PVBP utilization (approximately 9% of the IPS limit) leaves ample room to extend within the 3Y eligible band without approaching the PVBP ceiling. Existing fixed-rate STS placements become comparatively more attractive as reinvestment rates fall.

Yields −100bps

Prioritize locking in duration

A larger decline strengthens the case for extending toward the 3-year eligible maximum on any available headroom, and for pre-funding upcoming maturities into longer tenors before reinvestment rates fall further. A decline of this magnitude would also reduce the cost of the floating-rate Pak Oman facility, easing the negative carry on the COI pairing without requiring corrective action.

Yields +50bps

Maintain short duration bias

Continue rolling maturing proceeds into short-dated T-Bills (3M/6M) at upcoming auctions to capture the higher rate environment, rather than locking into 1-year paper. This reinforces the existing recommendation to avoid repeating the low-rate legacy 1-year locks. Rising yields would also increase the cost of the floating-rate Pak Oman facility, warranting closer monitoring of the COI pairing's carry position.

Yields +100bps

Maximize reinvestment velocity

Favor the shortest available tenors (overnight to 1-month) to preserve the ability to reprice into materially higher yields as they become available, and defer any STS or fixed-rate allocation until new placements can be made at the higher prevailing rate. A move of this magnitude would meaningfully worsen the Pak Oman facility's floating-rate cost, making a review of that pairing — and, if feasible, a partial reduction of the facility — a priority rather than a monitoring item.

8. Alpha Optimization & Reallocation Maximizing alpha within IPS constraints

Lever 1 — Gated on data

Grow STS allocation toward the 30% ceiling

STS placements yield a materially higher rate premium than COI or MTB (1Y) holdings. Illustratively, reallocating 5 percentage points of the portfolio from COI/1Y T-Bills into AA+-rated STS paper at current rates could contribute an estimated 10–15bp of incremental gross yield. This estimate is directional and contingent on ratings and sector-concentration data becoming available.

Lever 2 — Diversification

Activate Samba Bank capacity

Samba Bank currently sits at 0% against a permitted 2–80% band. Subject to availability, redirecting a portion of upcoming maturities into this counterparty at rates comparable to Faysal Bank (approximately 11.1–11.25%) would represent a modest, low-risk yield improvement while reducing single-counterparty concentration.

Lever 3 — Forward-looking

Reassess tenor selection on legacy maturities

Approximately PKR 2,585.7mn is committed at 10.55–10.75% through February–March 2027. On maturity, it is recommended that management evaluate the prevailing rate view before committing to a further full one-year tenor.
