Programme Summary Brief · PC-1 Submission

NEAGAP — National Export-Oriented Agricultural Finance Guarantee Programme

Public risk capital as catalytic risk-sharing for agri value-chain finance beyond the farm gate

June 2026  ·  NCGCL & Ministry of National Food Security & Research  ·  Confidential — Draft for Submission

1. The Problem NEAGAP Addresses

Pakistan's agricultural credit system is focused almost entirely at the farm gate — input finance, seasonal working capital, and commodity production. The infrastructure that transforms farm output into tradable value — aggregation, cold chain, processing, grading, and logistics — remains structurally underfinanced. Existing bank credit templates, built for hard collateral and predictable cashflows, do not accommodate the seasonal, contract-linked, and asset-light profile of value-chain enterprises. The result is a missing middle: PKR 200–300 billion in unmet financing across priority export value chains.

NEAGAP targets the layer where agricultural output becomes tradable value — between the farm and the market, where post-harvest loss, processing gaps, and logistics deficits destroy export potential.

2. Programme Structure — Capital Cascade

NEAGAP operates as a partial credit guarantee facility within NCGCL. Public risk capital and NCGCL second-loss capital are each leveraged 3× to generate guarantee coverage. At 25% coverage, the guaranteed capacity unlocks a multiple of private bank financing.

NEAGAP — Portfolio Design, Risk Framework & Deployment

4. Portfolio Allocation & Enterprise Sizing

NEAGAP is not a general-purpose agricultural credit guarantee. The portfolio is split between a Core Guarantee Support Window (≥70% — existing operating enterprises with demonstrated business activity) and a First-Mover/Strategic Window (≤30% — new enterprises and catalytic entry). A further sub-limit of ≤10% is reserved for larger sector-unlocking transactions, subject to Board/BRIC review.

5. Mechanism — How a Guarantee Facility Works
1
PFI Originates

Bank underwrites value-chain borrower on cash flow, contracts & collateral. NCGCL provides no markup subsidy.

2
Guarantee Issued

NCGCL issues partial guarantee covering 25% of credit exposure. PFI retains 75% — skin in the game preserved.

3
PFI Monitors

Financing partner originates, disburses, monitors, restructures & pursues recovery. NCGCL conducts portfolio review.

4
Loss (if any)

Public capital absorbs first 12.5%. NCGCL absorbs next 12.5%. Residual 75% falls on PFI.

5
Value Created

Incremental finance reaches enterprises that would otherwise remain unserved — additionality is the test.

6. Risk Framework & Controls
Sector capsPer-PFI, single-name, and vintage limits guard against concentration; ≤15% per lender at all times.
Portfolio windowsCore/first-mover split enforces additionality — 70% to operating enterprises with track records.
PFI discipline75% residual retention on every facility preserves lender accountability throughout the credit lifecycle.
Ticket sizingPKR 25M–150M band ensures administrative viability at minimum and catalytic relevance at maximum.
GovernanceBRIC approval required for anchor/platform transactions; Board visibility on portfolio composition quarterly.
Additionality testGuarantee coverage must demonstrably enable transactions that would not proceed on standalone bank terms.
RiskMitigation
Credit / counterpartyLayered loss absorption: public first-loss 12.5% → NCGCL second-loss 12.5% → 75% PFI residual; borrower limits; cashflow underwriting
ConcentrationSingle-name cap PKR 500M; per-PFI cap 15%; sector and sub-sector limits; vintage-year controls
Agri / commodity priceStructured as cashflow and contract-backed facilities; inventory and receivable finance reduces price timing risk
Governance / fiduciaryNCGCL governance framework; BRIC oversight; shareholder reporting; ring-fenced NEAGAP window
7. Recommendation

NCGCL proposes approval of PKR 1 Bn in public risk capital for a ring-fenced NEAGAP guarantee window, matched by PKR 1 Bn NCGCL second-loss capacity — generating PKR 6 Bn in guarantee issuance and PKR 24 Bn in mobilised private financing for agro-processing and value-chain upgrading beyond primary production. Governance operates through NCGCL's existing board and risk framework. NCGCL second-loss participation remains subject to applicable shareholder approvals.
