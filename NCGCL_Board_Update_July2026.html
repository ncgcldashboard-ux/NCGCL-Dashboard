Section A

Executive Snapshot

Portfolio metrics as at June 30, 2026 · Financial data YTD June 2026

Milestone — Second Institution Begins Issuance · HBL FI
Following May's first-ever guarantee to Kashf Foundation under PMIC, HBL FI has now issued guarantees to AGAHE and RCDP — the second institution to activate under its executed MAGFA, with further disbursements expected in July. NCGCL now has active exposure across two guarantee-issuing relationships (PMIC and HBL FI). Total guarantee revenue recognised YTD: PKR 487,785 (accrued fee income plus amortised PMIC upfront fee).
Milestone — PKR 1 Bn Budgetary Allocation Secured in Budget 2026-27
The Government of Pakistan's Budget 2026-27 has allocated PKR 1 Bn specifically for NCGCL to extend credit guarantees to export-oriented SMEs. This formalises fiscal-level recognition of NCGCL's mandate and provides a dedicated capital base to scale guarantee coverage in support of Pakistan's export sector — a significant validation of the Company's catalytic finance model at the national policy level.
Milestone — AAA / A1+ Credit Rating Renewed by PACRA
PACRA has renewed NCGCL's long-term rating of AAA and short-term rating of A1+, reaffirming the Company's highest-tier credit standing. The renewal underpins NCGCL's capacity to issue guarantees at scale and reinforces counterparty confidence across the FI, DFI and multilateral partner base.
Pipeline Milestone — PKR 19.3 Bn · +5.3% MoM Growth
Total quantified pipeline grew to PKR 19.3 Bn this month (33 active engagements, up from 31), driven by three newly quantified deals — Raqami (PKR 200 Mn), NAVTTC (PKR 200 Mn) and Taleem (PKR 45 Mn) — alongside continued advancement at Bank of Khyber (MoU signed, term sheet shared) and Bank of Punjab (in-principle approval, FI on-lending list finalised).

Guarantee Revenue Grows to PKR 487,785 — Two Institutions Now Contributing
With HBL FI's guarantees to AGAHE and RCDP now active alongside the PMIC/Kashf Foundation guarantee, the Company recognised total guarantee revenue of PKR 487,785 YTD — comprising accrued fee income on active guarantees plus the amortised portion of the PMIC upfront fee. This is the first period with guarantee income contributed by more than one issuing relationship.
Lower prevailing interest rates account for the YTD profit decline. YTD net profit of PKR 162.0 Mn is down 25% against prorated 2025 (PKR 216.2 Mn). This is attributable to lower interest rates prevailing during the year, which have compressed portfolio yields relative to 2025's higher-rate environment. Admin spend remains well under budget (21.9% favourable), and other income remains ahead of plan in absolute terms (+7% vs prorated 2025).

Section F

Investment Portfolio Performance

June 2026 Investment Review · Total portfolio PKR 9.69 Bn · Fixed income markets reverse May's selloff; yields ease broadly

Rates reversed lower; NCGCL sustained YTD outperformance despite a narrower monthly spread. Fixed income markets rallied in June as easing Middle East geopolitical tensions, softer oil prices, and improving liquidity pulled yields lower across the curve — the mirror image of May's selloff. NCGCL's weighted average portfolio yield stood at 11.35% against a benchmark of 11.07% (+28 bps), while the adjusted monthly return of 10.99% beat the peer fund average of 10.85% by +14 bps — a narrower margin than May's +94 bps, reflecting how broadly the whole market rallied together. On a YTD basis the picture strengthened further: NCGCL's YTD return of 9.92% now outperforms the peer average of 9.33% by +59 bps (up from +27 bps in May), and YTD portfolio yield of 9.92% beats the average benchmark of 9.40% by +52 bps.
