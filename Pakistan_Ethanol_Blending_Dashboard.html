PAKISTAN ETHANOL BLENDING STRATEGY

Cabinet-Ready Economic & Implementation Analysis | March 2026

Executive Summary

Pakistan faces a critical opportunity to reduce its $13 billion annual petrol import bill through strategic ethanol blending. By using domestic molasses (a sugar mill byproduct) for E5 blending and importing competitive ethanol for E10 blending, the nation can achieve $826 million in annual forex savings while reducing pump prices by 5-6% for consumers.

§2 Economic Scenarios: Three Pathways Analyzed
Scenario A: E5 Only (Domestic Ethanol)
Annual Forex Saving
$516 Million

Mechanism: 481.5 ML domestic ethanol (5% of 9,630 ML petrol) displaces 501.5 ML petrol import. Forex savings: $677M (petrol reduction) − $153M (ethanol cost) − $75M (infrastructure amortized) = $449M net annual benefit.

Pump Price: Rs 170/L (−Rs 5/L from Rs 175/L pure petrol). Consumer savings: Rs 30B/year.

Limitation: Does not leverage full benefit of E10 import option; leaves domestic capacity partially utilized (32% vs potential 64% at E10 levels).

Scenario B: E10 Only (All Imported)
Annual Forex Saving
$628 Million

Mechanism: 963 ML imported ethanol at $0.65/L (global market price). Forex savings: $1,354M (petrol reduction) − $626M (ethanol import cost) − $100M (infrastructure) = $628M net annual benefit.

Pump Price: Rs 167.50/L (−Rs 7.5/L). Consumer savings: Rs 50B/year.

Limitation: 100% import-dependent; no domestic capacity development; vulnerable to global ethanol price volatility; missed opportunity to incentivize mill distillery capex.

Scenario C: E5+E10 Concurrent (RECOMMENDED) ✓
Annual Forex Saving
$830 Million

Mechanism: 481.5 ML domestic (E5) + 481.5 ML imported (E10 gap) = 963 ML total. Forex savings: $1,354M (petrol) − $111M (domestic ethanol) − $313M (imported ethanol) − $100M (infrastructure) = $830M net annual benefit.

Pump Price: Rs 159/L average (progressive: Rs 170/L E5 Year 1 → Rs 167.50/L E10 Year 3). Consumer savings: Rs 60B/year by Year 3.

Strategic Advantages:

  • 60% higher savings than E5-only ($314M/year additional)
  • Hybrid supply (domestic + import) reduces volatility & import dependence
  • Creates mill distillery incentive: 60% annual ROI at E5 commitment level → natural capacity expansion
  • Phased vehicle rollout minimizes risk (E5 nationwide, E10 gradual)
  • 10-year cumulative: $6.8B benefit vs $3.9B (E5-only) = $2.9B premium

E5 Compatibility Summary

Finding: E5 is safe for 95%+ of fleet. No modifications required. Safe for 2-stroke motorcycles (which cannot use E10).

✓ RECOMMENDATION: Launch E5 nationwide immediately (Month 6). Zero vehicle risk. Minimum political resistance.

⚠ E10 Compatibility Risk
Problem: 60% of vehicles require fuel system upgrades to safely use E10 (corrosion of brass components, rubber seals, phase separation risk in hot storage).
Solution: Phased rollout. E5 nationwide (month 6). E10 premium pumps only (month 12-24); vehicle verification at pump. E5 as safety option always available.
Outcome: By 2035, 80% of fleet naturally E10-compatible through retirement of older vehicles. No forced upgrades; market-driven transition.

§6 Risk Assessment & Mitigation
Critical Risks (Ranked by Probability × Impact)
Vehicle Damage Claims (E10 on Pre-2010 Fleet)
Probability: Medium (20%) if E10 rolled out nationally without vehicle verification
Impact: High ($100M+ in repair claims)
Mitigation: Phase 2 pilot at premium pumps (month 12-24); vehicle year verification at pump; success threshold <1% damage claims
Owner: PSO/PARCO; Government insurance reserve (Rs 5B) covers repairs
Global Ethanol Price Spike
Probability: Medium (20%) if Brazil drought or India mandates restrict supply
Impact: Medium ($100-150M annual cost impact)
Mitigation: Forward contracts (12-24 months) at $0.65-0.70/L; price hedge clause (government absorbs if >$0.80/L, capped $50M/year)
Owner: Ministry Energy (procurement tenders)
Molasses Supply Shortfall
Probability: Low (10%) if drought impacts sugarcane production
Impact: Medium ($50-100M impact on ethanol availability)
Mitigation: Government buffer stock (50K MT capacity); import ethanol backup; 3-year mill forward contracts
Owner: NCGCL + Ministry Industries
Fuel Quality Inconsistency
Probability: Medium (15%) if PSQCA testing protocols weak
Impact: Low-Medium (reputation damage; warranty claims)
Mitigation: PSQCA lab capacity upgrade; 50-pump monthly sampling; strict compliance standards (ethanol 5.0±0.5%, water <0.2%)
Owner: PSQCA + PSO/PARCO
Crude Oil Price Crash
Probability: Low (15%) if global recession
Impact: Medium (benefit reduces; still positive)
Mitigation: Program remains economical until crude falls below $36-38/barrel (EIA forecasts $64-70 by 2027); triggers policy review
Owner: Cabinet (policy review)
