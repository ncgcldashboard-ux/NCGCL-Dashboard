CAR treatment

The loss model first calculates an economic-capital rate independently of CAR. Internal risk weight is calibrated against the Board reference CAR of 20%. The selected allocation CAR then determines the capital actually allocated: Internal RW × selected CAR × guarantee limit. Changing this input therefore updates allocated capital, capital utilisation and RAROC, while the underlying economic-capital rate remains unchanged.

5 · IFRS 9 financial guarantee engine

Staging, probability-weighted ECL, discounting and subsequent measurement

The engine measures ECL for issued financial guarantee contracts using the risk that the specified debtors default, expected guarantee payments net of incremental post-claim recoveries, forward-looking scenarios and discounting. The recognised guarantee liability is the higher of the ECL allowance and the unamortised initial-recognition amount.

Reporting and staging policy

Stage 2 is triggered by a significant increase in the risk of default since initial recognition, forward-looking qualitative indicators, restructuring/watchlist status, or the past-due backstop. Stage 3 is triggered by credit impairment/default.

Scenario methodology

Weights must sum to 100%. PD, LGD and expected utilisation multipliers are applied by scenario.

Accounting boundary

The management EL and economic-capital outputs above remain separate from IFRS 9. IFRS 9 uses 12-month ECL for Stage 1, lifetime ECL for Stage 2, a credit-impaired cash-shortfall estimate for Stage 3, and a separate subsequent-measurement comparison with the unamortised initial-recognition amount.

Measurement convention

For Stage 1, the model includes lifetime cash shortfalls arising from defaults possible in the next 12 months. For Stage 2, it includes defaults possible throughout the maximum remaining contractual exposure period. Gross claim payments and incremental post-claim recoveries are discounted separately to the reporting date.
