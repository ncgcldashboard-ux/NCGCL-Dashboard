HBL Zarai Services Limited — Portfolio Partial Trade Credit Guarantee

Credit Proposal (May 2026)

Facility Type
Portfolio-Level Partial Trade Credit Guarantee (Controlled Sandbox)
Underlying Portfolio
PKR 150 Mn (pilot receivables portfolio)
Tenor
2026 Cropping Season
Kharif & Rabi · ~180 days per cycle
NCGCL Max Contingent Liability
PKR 37.5 Mn (25% portfolio cover)
Purpose
Controlled deployment of HBL Zarai's deferred settlement / trade-credit receivables product across four Dera hubs in Punjab (Sahiwal, Pakpattan, Okara, Gujranwala), covering ~1,500 acres during 2026 Kharif and Rabi cropping seasons.
Repayment Source
Self-liquidating through harvest-linked settlement of the financed crop. Settlement pathways in order of preference:
  • Full in-kind net-off via produce delivery to Zarai's Dera
  • Partial in-kind net-off plus external surplus sale
  • Custody storage with deferred sale (max. 90 days)
  • Cash settlement following pre-approved external sale with NOC
NCGCL Exposure — Layered Attachment
Maximum contingent liability of PKR 37.5 Mn (25% of PKR 150 Mn pilot portfolio), comprising:
LayerLoss BandAllocationLoss Value (PKR Mn)Logic
1. Mandatory Deductible0–10%Zarai 100%15.0First-loss retention by originator
2. Pari Passu11–30%Zarai 50% / NCGCL 50%30.0Main risk-sharing band; PKR 15 Mn each
3. NCGCL Tail31–45%NCGCL 100%22.5Severe but eligible stress cover
4. Zarai Retained> 45%Zarai 100%> 67.5Residual risk; renewal review on breach
NCGCL's PKR 37.5 Mn cap = PKR 15 Mn (pari passu share) + PKR 22.5 Mn (tail layer).
Guarantee Coverage
25% portfolio-level coverage of HBL Zarai's trade-credit / deferred payments portfolio. Coverage is layered through a four-stage loss waterfall (see Layered Attachment above).
  • Tenor: 180 days from the invoice date
Cover applies only to eligible crystallized net losses after evidence gates and recoveries.
Pricing
  • Pari Passu Layer Fee: 3.00% p.a. on PKR 15 Mn pari passu exposure, payable quarterly, in arrears
  • Tail Layer Fee: 1.50% p.a. on PKR 22.5 Mn tail exposure, payable quarterly, in arrears
Fees are non-contingent on claim utilization.
Security / Risk Mitigants
  • Mandatory parametric crop insurance
  • Crop Delivery Commitment / Farmer Trade Credit Agreement enforceable under local law
  • Mandatory harvest mechanization using Zarai's machinery pooling
  • Crop intake and custody controls covering quantity verification, quality record, farmer-plot-receivable linkage, settlement rights, and chain-of-custody record
General Covenants
  • Compliance with SBP, SECP and insurance regulations, as applicable
  • Field visits at least once per week per grower with time-stamped GPS-tagged photographic evidence
  • Anti-collusion field-team rotation with bi-weekly customer list swap between Team A and Team B
  • NCGCL spot-visit access (minimum 25 growers, 6 per Dera, unannounced) and independent surveyor appointment rights
  • Monthly portfolio reporting; quarterly joint reviews
  • Five-part custody documentation prior to any net-off
  • Data integrity protocol with timestamps, and full audit trail
  • End-of-season review before continuation into second season / Rabi 2026
  • Zarai to seek prior NOC from NCGCL in case of any reduction in its shareholding held with HBL Group (currently 99.99%)
Financial Covenants
NCGCL to be notified within 7 working days of any of the following breaches:
  • DPD 90 > 5%
  • Side-selling > 5% of enrolled accounts
  • Single-buyer concentration: not applicable in the pilot phase to protect scale; to be capped at 20% in subsequent scaling-up phases
  • Maintenance of Zarai tangible net worth above an agreed floor
Breach may trigger portfolio review.
Policy Deviations
Material deviations identified against NCGCL's Guarantee Policy 2025, primarily relating to the treatment of HBL Zarai as a partner platform:
  • Zarai is not a regulated / licensed financial institution under SBP or SECP frameworks (§5.2(a))
  • Zarai's lending and operational track record is below the 3-year threshold (§5.1(a) & §9.2)
  • Zarai is unrated by a local credit rating agency (§5.2(e), Annexure C)
Counterparty Snapshot — HBL Zarai Services Limited
Wholly owned subsidiary of Habib Bank Limited (HBL) — 99.99% ownership; HBL is in turn a subsidiary of The Aga Khan Fund for Economic Development S.A. (AKFED). Incorporated October 2023 with a mandate to improve farmer economics through an integrated Dera-based agronomy, mechanization, inputs, and offtake bundle.
