03Why · The Underwriting Mismatch

The value-chain borrower does not fit the standard credit template

Banks avoid these transactions because they do not fit standard credit models: seasonal cash flows, limited collateral, specialized assets, inventory risk, and repayment tied to buyers rather than land.

What conventional credit underwrites

  • Hard collateral & predictable asset values
  • Visible & audited cashflows
  • Known sponsors with established & long track record
  • Fixed repayment cycles

What value-chain enterprises look like

  • Seasonal procurement cycles
  • Perishable and price-sensitive inventory
  • Receivables, utilization, and offtake risk
  • Specialized assets with weak secondary markets
The result is a missing middle: too formal and capital-intensive for informal finance; too under-collateralized, seasonal, and commercially unfamiliar for standard bank credit. The enterprises that bridge farms to markets remain chronically underfinanced.

Without credit coverage, financial institutions see unfamiliar risk. With disciplined risk-sharing, the same transaction can be underwritten on cash flow, contracts, collateral substitutes, and portfolio controls.

05How · The Capital Stack

50 percent guarantee coverage, absorbed through a defined waterfall

NEAGAP proposes coverage of up to 25 percent of eligible credit losses. The first 12.5 percent is absorbed by public risk capital layer; the next 12.5 percent is absorbed by NCGCL's existing risk capital. The PFI retains the residual 75 percent.

08What · Eligible Transaction Types

NEAGAP is not a crop loan dressed up as value-chain finance

The facility supports specific transaction types beyond the farm gate: receivables, working capital, inventory finance, infrastructure term debt, and service-platform finance. 11Risk · Facility Controls

The principal risks are identified, capped, and governed through the structure

NEAGAP's risk framework is built into the facility design: layered loss absorption, PFI residual exposure, borrower limits, sector limits, eligibility screening, documentation standards, vintage monitoring, and governance-triggered portfolio actions.

RiskLIStructural mitigation
CreditMHLayered loss absorption through public first-loss and NCGCL second-loss structure; 75% PFI residual exposure; borrower limits; cash-flow and contract-based underwriting; recovery discipline.
ConcentrationMHSingle-name cap; PFI exposure cap; sector and geography limits; strategic-window sub-limit; periodic BRIC review.
OperationalMMExecution on NCGCL's existing platform; approved facility documents; defined eligibility rules; reporting templates; staged PFI onboarding.
Commodity priceMHNo direct price support; repayment linked to offtake, receivables, inventory, or value-added activity; sector diversification.
Climate & natural disasterHHResilience screening; regional and value-chain diversification; storage, cooling, and infrastructure standards where applicable.

13 · Recommendation · Action Item Proposed

Recommend allocation of PKR 1 Bn in public risk capital

NCGCL proposes approval of PKR 1 Bn in public risk capital for a ring-fenced NEAGAP guarantee window within NCGCL, to be deployed as first-loss risk capacity to mobilize private financing for value-chain upgrading beyond primary production.

The proposed structure will operate through NCGCL governance, with public risk capital absorbing the first-loss layer, NCGCL existing risk capital absorbing the second-loss layer subject to applicable approvals, and participating financial institutions retaining residual exposure on every facility.
